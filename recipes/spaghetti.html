Spaghetti Bolognese: Traditional Pasta Recipe

Spaghetti bolognese is a traditional Italian recipe made of tomato sauce, minced mear and milk, that is its secret ingredient. But what is the difference between bolognese and meat sauce? They're essentially the same, but bolognese sauce is thicker and creamier, while meat sauce is an American meat-based recipe mostly open to variations.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti (400g)
  • Lean Pork, Minced (100g)
  • Veal Stifle, Minced (200g)
  • Carrot (1)
  • Onion (1)
  • White Wine (100ml)
  • Tomato Sauce or Fried Tomatoes (200g)
  • Milk (50ml)
  • Oregano
  • Basil Leaves, To Garnish
  • Olvie Oil
  • Salt and Freshly Ground Black Pepper To Taste

Steps

  1. Peel the onion and carrot, then chop them finely. Pour a little oil into the pot and set it over medium-low heat.
  2. When the oil is hot, add the onion and the carrot together with the salt and cook them for about 10 minutes or until tender and barely brown.
  3. Add the minced meat and mix it using a spatula so that it does not stick together in big pieces. Add the salt, freshly ground black pepper and oregano to taste.
  4. When the meat is no longer pink, increase the heat to high, add the white wine and wait for the alcohol to evaporate, it will only take 2-3 minutes.
  5. Lower the heat, then add the tomato sauce and the milk. Let the sauce simmer for 20 minutes.
  6. When the bolognese sauce is almost ready you can make the past. Put abundant water to boil in a pot over high heat.
  7. When the water boils, add a teaspoon of salt and the spaghetti, and as soon as the water boils again start counting the minutes indicated on the package.
  8. When the spaghetti is ready, add the drained spaghetti to the bolognese sauce with the fire off. Mix will.
  9. Serve the spaghetti bolognese with a few basil leaves. Enjoy!
